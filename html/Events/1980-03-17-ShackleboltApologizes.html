03/17/1980: Shacklebolt Apologizes

Summary:

Sirius announces that Kingsley Shacklebolt has just arrived at Grimmauld Place and wants to apologize. Sirius believes Kingsley, but also threatened to cut him off from Sanctuary if he insulted Regulus again. They have a brief tangent where Sirius insists that Regulus call them 'Siri', not 'Sirius'. Regulus agrees to talk to Kingsley, but only if James comes with him.

Once downstairs, Kingsley explains that he's spent years working with 'victims of Death Eater brutality', specifically The Artist's burn victims. There's a brief tangent where Regulus explains that he didn't know about the recovery process, but isn't shocked that Bellatrix liked fire, as she didn't know when to stop her torture. Kingsley states that he now firmly believes that Regulus is, in fact, a victim, whereas he was originally inclined to see him as a perpetrator.

Kingsley explains how Victim Advocacy came to be involved in Sanctuary, and that originally, the Lestrange Manor investigation wasn't connected to the Death Eater investigation. He says that Victim Advocacy was mostly focused on getting Pet out, and that he personally presented several plans to Moody. He then explains that all of the Lestrange Manor Silenced are having a difficult time dealing with the after-effects of their captivity.

Kingsley recounts Pet's treatment at Lestrange Manor, believing Regulus to be unaware of the details. Regulus states that he's aware, but will not reveal Pet's identity to Shacklebolt. Regulus explains that he refers to Pet using it/its pronouns because when acting as Pet, it's not human. He claims that he's protecting Pet's identity because he doesn't trust that the secret will remain private.

Regulus asks Kingsley if he can try to start trusting him. Kingsley agrees, and Regulus says that he'll try to trust Kingsley in return.
